Article Title

My name is Dr. Richard Smith, a postdoctoral researcher at NOIRLab's Gemini North Observatory in Hilo, Hawaii. My current research focuses on variable stars in the Andromeda Galaxy using Hubble Space Telescope data. I am using a combination of light curve and difference imaging analysis to identify and characterise different types of variable stars. In the most recent publication from this study, my collaborators and I identified, confirmed and classified variable stars in stellar clusters within M31. We were able to classify these cluster variable stars as a result of previously obtained age and metallicity estimates for their host clusters, allowing for cluster isochrones to be produced. We therefore utilized the observed variability of M31 cluster stars and, for the first time, their isochrone-derived stellar properties to identify their variable star classes.

 

I moved to Hilo in August 2023 after graduating from my doctoral program at Queen's University Belfast, Northern Ireland, in July 2023. My thesis investigated the properties of trans-Neptunian Binaries using archival Hubble Space Telescope observations. At the heart of this study was the development and application of a sophisticated point spread function-fitting algorithm capable of accurately measuring the position and brightness values of both binary components simultaneously.
